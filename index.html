Frequently Asked Questions

Intelligence is the buzz word and we provide you answers to some of the most asked questions related to this event.

Hackathon? What?

It's just an event, where programmers meet to engage in collaborative computer programming environment. In short, a full dose of fun.

Who can come?

Zero to One Hackathon applications are open to any college student, currently pursuing higher education in India.

Can I Afford it?

Hack Zero to One costs only ₹149/participant. We provide you free meals, caffeine, swag, workspace, and WiFi for the event.

What Should I Bring?

All you need is a valid student ID and a laptop. Rest is our headache. Pillow Bag is recommended though.

Any doubt?

What's the Plan?

It's always good to know about the event's schedule, so you can do some handy preparations before the event.

Day 1

9:00

Event Starts. Teams arrive and get their respective IDs. Workspace is alloted as well.

10:00

The real challenge begins. Teams brainstorm to discuss the plan, work flow and task assignment to start the development process.

13:00

After the event has picked up the pace, here comes the time for first Keynote talk.

Day 2

10:00

Development and Implementation time is up and the final product is submitted for evaluation.

11:00

Flash talk sessions and Panel discussions are held where final products are reviewed and revealed to the audience.

15:00

Results are declared and winning products are faciliated with prizes. Happy Ending with closing notes.

About The Organiser

This event is coordinated by Computer Engineering Student Society (CESS) of Computer Engineering Department of Guru Nanak Dev University, Amritsar, India, which is ran by students under the guidance of Dr. Sandeep Sharma as a Head of Department. CESS being a student organisation, handle all the major events of the department. In October 2018, CESS orgainsed a mega Polaris TechFest, and it was a proud moment for the University.
